So What Makes a Good Designer?

A good designer is someone who not only considers the needs of the business or company, but also the needs of their customers. We are the advocates and evangelists for our users.

Design is a balancing act. A good designer must be able to think things through analytically and holistically — solving specific or small problems without forgetting about the larger picture — it's the micro and the macro.

A good designer also has humility — leave your ego at the door. It’s not about you or I, it’s about us and we. It doesn’t matter who was right or wrong, it only matters that we’re doing our best. Even the smartest and most experienced designers won’t have all the answers, sometimes we need to explore and test. It's important to remember, you don’t have to fail to succeed, but you can’t succeed if you’re not willing to fail.

What Makes a Good Team?

In this fast paced world, it’s how your team, handles and adapts to changes. A good team is agile and flexible — they understand the whole process. You won’t always know what will work and what doesn’t. Iterating quickly allows you to see what sticks and what doesn’t. If it’s bad it should be trashed. The work might be lost but result is we learned. A great team knows to continually move forward.
